16 beats

Teen Taal (तीन ताल)

The most popular taal in Hindustani music. 16 matras in 4 equal vibhags.

10 beats

Jhap Taal (झप ताल)

A 10-beat taal in 2+3+2+3. Widely used in classical vocal compositions.

12 beats

Ek Taal (एक ताल)

12-beat taal in 6 equal vibhags of 2. Essential in kheyal compositions.

7 beats

Rupak Taal (रूपक ताल)

Unique 7-beat taal with sam on khali (3+2+2). Used widely in bhajans.

6 beats

Dadra Taal (दादरा ताल)

Light 6-beat taal in 3+3. Foundation of thumri and folk music traditions.

8 beats

Keherwa Taal (कहरवा ताल)

8-beat taal — the backbone of devotional and folk music across India. Great for beginners.

14 beats

Deepchandi Taal (दीपचंदी ताल)

14-beat taal in 3+4+3+4 arrangement. Commonly used in thumri and semi-classical forms.

12 beats

Chartal (चारताल)

12-beat taal in 2+2+2+2+2+2. Frequently used in dhrupad and kirtan compositions.

16 beats

Tilwada Taal (तिलवाड़ा ताल)

Slow 16-beat taal preferred in slow-tempo kheyal and classical performances.

Your Journey

From Beginner to Performer

A clear, structured path through the world of tabla — designed for every age and pace.

1

Introduction to Tabla

Learn the anatomy of the tabla — dayan, bayan, pudi, and syahi. Understand proper sitting posture and hand placement before touching the drum.

2

Basic Bols (Syllables)

Master the fundamental strokes: Dha, Dhin, Na, Tin, Te, Ge. These bols are the building blocks of every composition you will ever play.

3

First Taal — Teen Taal

Apply your bols to the 16-beat Teen Taal cycle. Start with the basic theka, then gradually layer in variations and speed.

4

Explore More Taals

Progress through Jhap Taal, Ek Taal, Rupak Taal, Keherwa, and beyond. Each taal opens up entirely new musical expressions.

5

Compositions & Performance

Learn full kayadas, relas, tihais, and gats. Build a rich repertoire and perform with confidence in any setting.
